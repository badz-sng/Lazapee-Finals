Quick Hummus Recipe

This recipe makes quick, tasty hummus, with no messing. It has been adapted from a number of different recipes that I have read over the years. Hummus is a delicious thick paste used heavily in Greak and Middle Eastern Dishes. It is very tasty with salad, grilled meat and pitta bread.

Ingredients

  • 1 Can(400mg) of chick peas (garbanzo beans)
  • 175g of tahini
  • 6 sundried tomatoes
  • Half a red pepper
  • A pintch of cayenne pepper
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • A dash of olive oil

Instructions

  1. Remove the skin of the garlic, and chop coarsely
  2. Remove all the seeds and stalk from the pepper, and chop coarsely
  3. Add all the Ingredients into a food processor
  4. Process all the ingredients into a paste
    • If you want a coarse "chunky" hummus, process it for a short time
    • If you want a smooth hummus, process it for a longer time

For a different flabour, you could try blending in a small measure of lemmon and coriander, chilli pepper, lime and chipotle, harissa and mint, or spinach and feta cheese. Experiment and see what works for you

Storage

Refrigirate the finished hummus in a sealed container. You should be able to use it for about a week after you've made it. If it starts to become fizzy, you should difinitely discard it.

Hummus is suitable for freezing; you should thaw is and use it within a couple of months
